The Verdicts

01

The Toss

Winning The Toss Means Almost Nothing

Toss winners win 50.5% of matches — barely better than a coin flip. But that headline hides everything that matters.

Choose to BAT → 44.3% wins Choose to FIELD → 53.7% wins

The toss itself is neutral. The decision is where the edge lives — a 9.4% swing between the right and wrong call.

02

Phase Analysis

Death Overs Decide Matches. Not Powerplays.

Everyone fixates on powerplay starts. The data disagrees.

Powerplay gap: +0.95 RPO Middle gap: +0.95 RPO Death gap: +1.77 RPO

Winners outscore losers by 1.77 runs per over in the death — nearly twice the powerplay gap. Score 50+ in the last 3 overs: 55% win rate. Score under 30: 14%.

03

Hidden Pattern

Over 7 Is The Free Wicket Nobody Takes

The moment the powerplay ends, scoring collapses.

Over 6: 8.70 RPO Over 7: 6.99 RPO ← the dead zone Over 1: 6.29 RPO

Over 7 is cheaper than the opening over. Captains who bowl their best spinner in the powerplay are wasting them — Over 7 is where wickets come for free.

04

Chase Difficulty

The Wall Is At 175, Not 200

People treat 200 as the unassailable target. The real cliff is earlier.

Chasing 150s: 72% success Chasing 160s: 50% — coin flip Chasing 170s: 40% Chasing 200+: 23%

The tipping point is ~175 runs. Every run above 170 is worth more than one below it — which makes death-over bowlers the most undervalued asset in the IPL.

05

Momentum

Momentum Is Real. Captains Ignore It.

After a 12+ run over, the next over averages 9.5 runs. After a quiet (<6) over, it averages 7.8 runs.

Big → Big: 30.5% chance Quiet → Big: 18.5% chance

That's a 12% momentum edge the batting side carries into the next over. A captain who doesn't change their bowling after conceding 15 is making a statistically measurable mistake.

06

Era Shift

Pre-2022 IPL Records Are In A Different Sport

The scoring acceleration since 2022 has been historic.

2008: 8.31 RPO · 10.7 sixes/match 2026: 9.81 RPO · 18.7 sixes/match

Sixes nearly doubled. Kohli's record 973-run season in 2016 was set when 8.3 RPO was elite. Today that's below average. Context is everything when comparing across eras.

07

CSK Anomaly

CSK Win The Toss At 60% — Regardless Of What They Choose

When CSK wins the toss and bats: they win 60.0% of the time.
When CSK wins the toss and fields: they win 60.0% of the time.

Exact same number. CSK's toss advantage isn't about the coin — it's about their ability to execute either way. That's not luck. That's culture.
